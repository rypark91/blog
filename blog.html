My Story

I always wanted to make games for a long time. So my journey to be a developer began in high school. It was my senior year, and I was suggested by my counselor to take a tech class, which I did.

I made the fatal mistake of learing from community college professors who took the whole sink or swim phiosophy to an extreme.

I got accepted at the University of Utah and left SLCC. After a semester of getting used to bigger college life, I decided to try and get into the computer science program. In fact, there was a game development program that is actually an emphasis for either computer science or film at the time. I took some classes attempted to get into the program twice and failed. So I decided to retake on of my past classes to raise my GPA. It still wasn't high enough, but I still pleaded my case. I finally got accepted in a program that's one of hardest to get in.

Once I got into the program, I went through college life. I have taken a variety of classes. Some not too bad, and others borderline spirit breaking. There were times when I had to retake some classes. Still I did not give up. my senior year, I finished up my final classes, and walked during graduation. I raised my fist in victory.

So began my hunt for a job and a place in the world. But I saw there is so much other things that were required despite me gaining the skills in college. To make matters worse, apparantly I didn't get the credit on one of my classes. So I pleaded for my case, proofed that my final project worked, and got requalified for graduation. When I'm not coding, I work at a cabinet shop to earn money. So, that's where I am and where I've been.
